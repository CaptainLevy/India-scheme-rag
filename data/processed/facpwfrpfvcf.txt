Financial Assistance for Construction/ Purchase of Wooden/ FRP Fishing Vessel (Canoe) for Fishermen OkYou need to before applying for schemesCancelSign In GoaFinancial Assistance for Construction/ Purchase of Wooden/ FRP Fishing Vessel (Canoe) for FishermenFinancial AssistanceFisheriesFishermanMarineSubsidyDetailsThe scheme Financial Assistance for Construction/ Purchase of Wooden/ FRP Fishing Vessel (Canoe) for Fishermen is a scheme by the Department of Fisheries, Government of Goa. Through this scheme, 50% of the actual cost of the Wooden/ Fiber-Reinforced Plastic (FRP) Fishing Vessel (Canoe) purchased by the fishermen is covered by the Government of Goa. The applicant will be entitled to a subsidy grant once every four years on one craft per family household.BenefitsFor General Category, 50% subsidy, limited to 60,000/-For SC/ST Category, 50% subsidy, limited to 80,000/-The applicant will be entitled to a subsidy grant once every four years on one craft per family household.EligibilityThe applicant should be a traditional fisherman by birth or profession.The applicant should be a resident of Goa.The applicant or any member of his household should not be a defaulter with the Directorate of Fisheries, Goa.The canoe purchased through the scheme should be duly registered with the Directorate of Fisheries under the Marine Fishing Regulation Act (MFR) Act 1980/ Merchant Shipping (MS) Act.The applicant shall not sell, lease, mortgage, or transfer the wooden/ FRP craft for 5 years from the date of purchase of the said wooden/FRP craft.The wooden/FRP craft purchased should have been operated exclusively for fishing purposes only.Application ProcessOfflineStep 1: The interested applicant should take print of the prescribed format of the application form.Step 2: In the application form, fill in all the mandatory fields, paste the passport-sized photograph (signed across, if required), and attach copies of all the mandatory documents (self-attest, if required).Step 3: Submit the duly filled and signed application form along with the documents to the concerned authority at the Department of Fisheries, Dayanand Bandodkar Marg, Patto Colony, Panaji Goa - 403 521, or to the official posted at BDO Office/ Sub-Offices of the Department of Fisheries. Grievance RedressalAny dissatisfaction or complaint about any service or its standards can be registered with the Department's Grievance Redressal Officer. The grievance can also be lodged on the link dir-fish.goa@nic.in. All complaints will be acknowledged by us and final action taken will be communicated within 30 days.Documents RequiredCertificate of Vessel Registration, issued by the Directorate of Fisheries, Government of Goa, under the Merchant Shipping Act 1958.Fishing Vessel License CertificateFishing Net License Certificate.Copy of Fishing Netbook (Updated)Original GST Bill from the Authorized Dealer towards the Construction of the Wooden / FRP Craft.Caste Certificate (ST/ SC) if applicable.Copy of Aadhar Card Copy of Election Card Copy of Bank Passbook/Statement (Aadhaar linked)Bank letter (if bank financed)Inspection Report along with photographs of Fishing Canoe, Net, and its Accessories by Fisheries Superintendent/ Fisheries Officer, Assistant Superintendent of Fisheries (As per format).Frequently Asked QuestionsCan the Fishing Canoe be financed through a bank, and what documents are required in such cases?Yes, bank financing is allowed. Documents include the bank passbook/statement (Aadhaar linked) and a bank letter if financed by the bank. Is it mandatory to possess a fishing vessel (trawler/purse- seiner) for eligibility, and what is the default status criterion?Applicants and their family members should not possess a fishing vessel (trawler/purse-seiner) and should not be defaulters of the Fisheries Department. How does the applicant affirm the purpose of using the Wooden/FRP Canoe and Nets purchased through the scheme?The applicant declares the intended use for fishing activity only in the self- declaration. Can you elaborate on the self-declaration process and the specific declarations required from the applicant?The self- declaration covers ownership, subsidy history, family members' details, usage of the Canoe, absence of fishing vessel registration, and non-default status. What role does the Inspection Report play, and what details does it encompass?The Inspection Report includes details on the Canoe's registration, owner, fishing details, engine, nets, life-saving equipment, ice box, and overall status. What is the inspection process, and who conducts the inspection for the Fishing Canoe under this scheme?Inspection is done by the Fisheries Superintendent/Officer, Assistant Superintendent of Fisheries, as per the specified format. What are the contact details for the Directorate of Fisheries in Goa, and how can applicants reach out for inquiries?The contact details are Dayanand Bandodkar Marg, Panaji Goa; Tel: (O) 2224838, (Dir) 2227780; Email: dir-fish.goa@nic.in; Website: www.fisheries.goa.gov.in. Can you provide details about the required Life Saving Equipments and Ice Box for the Fishing Canoe?Details include Life Buoy, Life Jacket, and specifications for the Ice Box, including liters, number, and cost. How is the subsidy amount calculated for SC/ST Category beneficiaries, and what is their maximum limit?SC/ST Category beneficiaries receive a 50% subsidy, limited to 80,000 under the scheme. What are the prerequisites for the Wooden/FRP craft to be considered for subsidy, particularly regarding its usage?The wooden/FRP craft purchased should have been operated exclusively for fishing purposes. What are the criteria for eligibility regarding the applicant's default status with the Directorate of Fisheries, Goa?The applicant or any household member should not be a defaulter with the Directorate of Fisheries, Goa. What restrictions are placed on the sale or transfer of the purchased wooden/FRP craft, and for how long?The applicant cannot sell, lease, mortgage, or transfer the craft for five years from the date of purchase under the scheme. Can a non-resident of Goa apply for the scheme, and what specific residency criteria must an applicant meet?No, only residents of Goa are eligible. The applicant must be a traditional fisherman by birth or profession and a resident of Goa. How often can an applicant receive a subsidy grant for a craft under this scheme, and what is the specified time frame?An applicant is entitled to a subsidy grant once every four years for one craft per family household under the scheme. What is the maximum subsidy limit for the purchase of a Wooden/FRP Fishing Vessel (Canoe) under the scheme for General Category beneficiaries in Goa? 60,000 is the maximum subsidy limit for General Category beneficiaries under the scheme for purchasing a Wooden/FRP Fishing Vessel (Canoe).
