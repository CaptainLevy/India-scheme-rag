(Horticulture) M.Sc. (Horticulture) Ph.D. (Horticulture) courses. The department introduced the scheme to ensure that students pursuing Horticulture degrees should have the right to quality education and support their education in order to help achieve their maximum potential.BenefitsUnder the scheme, scholarships/Stipends will be given to students according to the course/programme in which they are enrolled.Time frame: Applicant shall be awarded the stipend for a fixed number of years as follows:B.Sc. (Horticulture): 4 YearsM.Sc. (Horticulture): 2 YearsPh.D. (Horticulture): 3 YearsEligibilityApplicant should be an indigenous inhabitant of Nagaland.The students who are pursuing B.Sc. Hons. (Horticulture), M.Sc. Horticulture and Ph. D. Horticulture are eligible to apply.The institution in which the applicant is currently studying must be recognized by UGC.Applicant should not be receiving any form of financial assistance in the form of a Scholarship/Stipend from any other sources.Application ProcessOnlineOnline - via CSCsAll eligible students shall apply through the Common Scholarship Portal: https://scholarship.nagaland.gov.in Step 01: RegistrationTo register, visit the official Common Scholarship Portal of Nagaland: https://scholarship.nagaland.gov.in Create an Account: If the applicant is a new user, click on the "Register" option. Provide your MOBILE Number linked with AADHAAR to get an OTP and verify the mobile. Once Mobile is verified, provide essential details such as your name, email address and create a unique password. Click on the "Register" button to proceed.Step 02: LoginLogin to Your Account: Once your mobile number is verified, log in to your scholarship account using the credentials the applicant created during registration.Step 03: Fill in the User ProfilePersonal Details: Update your personal information, including name, date of birth, gender, category, Bank Account Details, etc.Step 04: Apply for ScholarshipSelect your Scholarship Scheme, Institution, Institution State, Institution, District and proceed.Academic Information: Provide details about your educational background, such as school/college name, course, year of study, etc.Step 05: Upload DocumentsUpload Required Documents: Depending on the scholarship applicants are applying for, applicants will need to upload supporting documents. Commonly required documents include:Step 06: Verify and SubmitReview Your Application: Carefully review all the information applicants have entered and the documents applicants have uploaded. Make sure everything is accurate and complete.Submit Application: Once applicants are satisfied with your application, click on the "Submit" button.Step 07: Track Your ApplicationApplication Tracking: After submitting your application, applicants can track its status on the portal: https://scholarship.nagaland.gov.in/ Log in to your account and check for updates on your application's status regularly.Note: Applications with incomplete/unclear documents will be summarily rejected.Documents RequiredPassport-size photographAadhaar CardInstitution Verification Form A copy of Aadhaar CardFirst page of Bank passbook or statement clearly indicating Account Number and Account Holders NameIndigenous CertificateEducational Qualification CertificatesCaste/community Certificate, if applicableDisability certificate, if applicableAny other documents as requiredFrequently Asked QuestionsWho is eligible for the State Horticulture Scholarship?Applicants must be indigenous inhabitants of Nagaland pursuing B.Sc. Hons. (Horticulture), M.Sc. Horticulture, or Ph.D. Horticulture in UGC-recognized institutions.What is the purpose of the scheme?The scholarship aims to provide financial assistance to students pursuing Horticulture degrees to support their education and help them achieve their maximum potential.What are the benefits of the scheme?Students will receive scholarships or stipends according to the course/programme they are enrolled in, for a fixed number of years: 4 years for B.Sc. (Horticulture), 2 years for M.Sc. (Horticulture), and 3 years for Ph.D. (Horticulture).Can students from outside Nagaland apply for this scholarship?No, only indigenous inhabitants of Nagaland are eligible for the State Horticulture Scholarship.Is there any restriction on the type of institutions eligible for this scholarship?Yes, the institution where the applicant is currently studying must be recognized by the University Grants Commission (UGC).Can students pursuing other horticulture-related courses apply for this scholarship?No, only students pursuing B.Sc. Hons. (Horticulture), M.Sc. Horticulture, or Ph.D. Horticulture are eligible.Are there any conditions regarding previous scholarships or financial aid?Applicants should not be receiving any other form of financial assistance in the form of scholarship or stipend from any other sources.How can I apply for the Scholarship?All eligible students shall apply through the Common Scholarship Portal: https://scholarship.nagaland.gov.inSources And ReferencesGuidelinesCommon Scholarship PortalOkWas this helpful?News and UpdatesNo new news and updates availableShare OkYou need to before applying for schemesCancelSign In NagalandState Horticulture ScholarshipBScHorticultureMScPhDScholarshipStipendStudentDetailsBenefitsEligibilityApplication ProcessDocuments RequiredFrequently Asked QuestionsThe State Horticulture Scholarship is a scholarship scheme, launched by the Department of Horticulture, Government of Nagaland to provide scholarship/stipends to students who are pursuing B.Sc.
